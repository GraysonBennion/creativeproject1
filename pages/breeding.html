Why You Should Care About Your Pokemon's Parents

IVs or Individual Values, are what constitutes your Pokemon's genetics. Some Pokemon are born stronger than others, plain and simple. There might be two caterpies that have lived the exact same life, they have the same natures and number of EVs, but one will always be faster than the other. This would be because the faster caterpie has a higher number of IVs in it's speed stat.

A Pokemon can have 31 IVs in a given stat which are added to the "baseline" for the species. In other words a Pokemon with all 0's for IV's is the worst of it's kind and a Pokemon with 31's for IV's is the best of it's kind (at least for battling).

To prevent you from having to go out and catch 600 Caterpies in the hopes that you will find one with perfect IV's, the game provides a method to pass on IV's from parents to children in the from of breeding. The daycare center does a lot more than just allow your Pokemon to gain levels, it also allows you to create very powerful Pokemon.

Depending on which game you are in there are unique ways to do this, but across the board you are able to pass on good stats the children of parent Pokemon. As a rule, when two Pokemon breed, the baby will always belong to the mother's species. This means that it isn't important and much which species the father is, as long as the two parents are from compatible egg groups (there information can be found online).

Basically what you need to remember is that if you have a male Charizard with high Attack IVs, and a female Charizard with high HP IVs then there are special items you can give them to hold in the daycare so that they will pass on those stats to the baby Chamander.

Happy Breeding!
