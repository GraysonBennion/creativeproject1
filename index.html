Welcome to The World of (Competitive) Pokemon!

Welcome to the website, thanks for visiting! My hope is that this site can be a valuable resource for those people wishing to know more about how to play the Pokemon video games in the competitive sphere.

As an introduction for those who aren't aware, there are many numbers and algorithms involved when calculating stats for different pokemon. There are methods in the games to manipulate these numbers to get the best result for the Pokemon you want to battle with. Imagine you have an Alakazam and you want it to be really powerful at using the attack "Psychic". As a general rule, Alakazam's have a very high special attack stat, which is great because that will affect Psychic's Power.

There's a lot to talk about in competitive Pokemon, but for now I will only talk about how to maniuplate a Pokemon's stats for the greatest effect. In the example of Alakazam, depending on some factors, your level 100 Alakazam could have a special attack stat of anywhere from 247 all the way to 336, almost 100 points higher! The techniques you can learn from this website will help you to create the type of Pokemon that YOU want to battle with. Maybe you don't want an attacking Alakazam as much as a defensive one? We can help you do that.

Every Pokemon species has a "baseline" stat spread. This just means that certain species are better are certain things. Alakazams all have naturally higher speed and special attack, for example. Beyond that there are only a handful of other elements that make up a particular Pokemon's stats. Some I won't be talking about are held items and abilities, but these speak for themselves for the most part in the games. What the games don't tell you about are EV's, IV's (explained in the breeding section) and Natures.
